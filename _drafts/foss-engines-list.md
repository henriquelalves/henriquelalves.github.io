On the years I tried my hand at Game Development, I came along quite a number of good proprietary Engines that would make my life so much easier at that; only recently (with the rise of mobile and HTML5 exportability) that I would start giving focus to FOSS Engines, for both moral and development reasons. I'll try to summarize on this list the Good (and Bad?) Engines I came accross, with some insights on how easy the Engine will be for the end-user and for those who wish to contribute for it.

What I characterize as a FOSS Game Engine (or, what all elements on this list have in common):
- Has a FOSS compatible lisense (MIT, Apache, etc.)
- Has an IDE to help the development of the software and integration of tools.
- Has the common ground of usable tools to create games easily accessable (sound and graphics output, user input, physics optional).
- Has the capability of exporting the software created for other platforms (at the very least to play as a native stand-alone software).
